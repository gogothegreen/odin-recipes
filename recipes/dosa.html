Dosa recipe

Description

Dosa is a popular south Indian pancake-shaped breakfast dish. It is made with a fermented lentils and rice batter. Dosas are usually thin and crispy, and are served with chutney or sambar.

Ingredients

  • Raw rice
  • Cooked rice
  • Huskless split black lentils
  • Salt
  • Fenugreek seeds

Steps

Preparing the batter

  1. Soak 2 cups of raw rice in water for 6–7 hours.
  2. Similarly, soak 1 cup of split black lentils for 6–7 hours. Also add 1 tablespoon of fenugreek seeds. This helps with the frmentation.
  3. Grind the soaked rice and lentils separately. You can add 1 cup of cooked rice with the raw rice. This makes the dosas very soft.
  4. Mix these in a large container. As the fermented batter can expand to 2–3 times its volume, ensure that the container is large enough.
  5. Add half a teaspoon of salt and mix well.
  6. Let it ferment for 6–12 hours, depending on the temperature of your environment.

Making dosas

  1. Heat a flat pan. If non-stick. spread a thin layer of oil on top.
  2. First, mix the batter thouroughly. Spread the batter on the pan. You can use a ladle and spread it using a circular motion, starting from the inside to the outside of the pan.
  3. As the bottom gets cooked, the edges will start to peel-off the pan's surface, 40-90 seconds on medium high flame. Now, flip it.
  4. Wait 20-30 seconds for the other side to cook.
  5. Take it off the pan. Sertve hot n crispy dosas with chutney or sambar. You can fill it up with a potato masala to make Masala dosa!
